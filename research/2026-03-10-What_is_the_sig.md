The name Irene carries significant meaning rooted in ancient Greek language and mythology, primarily symbolizing "peace."

Here's a breakdown of its significance:

*   **Etymological Origin:** The name Irene is derived from the Greek word *eirēnē* (εἰρήνη), which directly translates to "peace."
*   **Mythological Significance:** In Greek mythology, Eirene (Irene) was the goddess of peace, often depicted as one of the Horae (or Hours), who were deities of the seasons and natural order. She was a daughter of Zeus and Themis, and her sisters included Dike (Justice) and Eunomia (Good Order). Eirene was also associated with wealth and harmony, and statues often depicted her holding Plutus, the god of wealth, in her arms. Her Roman counterpart is Pax.
*   **Historical and Cultural Impact:**
    *   **Early Christianity:** The name gained popularity among early Christians, symbolizing tranquility and harmony, and several saints bore the name Irene.
    *   **Byzantine Empire:** Irene was particularly prominent during the Byzantine Empire, with several empresses carrying the name, including Empress Irene of Athens, who ruled in the 8th century and played a key role in restoring the veneration of icons.
    *   **Spread Across Europe:** The name was adopted into Latin as "Irene" and spread throughout Europe, maintaining its association with peace and harmony. It has variations in many languages, such as Irena (Polish, Czech), Irina (Russian, Romanian), and Irène (French).
*   **Symbolism:** Beyond its literal meaning, Irene symbolizes unity, goodwill, serenity, and harmony. It is often associated with positive sentiments and a gentle, intellectual character in literature and everyday life.
