Lendlease Australia is currently undergoing a significant strategic repositioning, with its 2026 outlook focused on strengthening its core Australian business and global investment platform while exiting international development and construction. The company released its half-year FY2026 results on February 23, 2026, which revealed a statutory loss after tax of $318 million, reflecting the "transitional year" as it implements its May 2024 strategic overhaul.

For FY2026, Lendlease anticipates its Investments, Development, and Construction (IDC) segment to contribute earnings per security (EPS) of 28 to 34 cents, with a stronger performance expected in the second half of the fiscal year. A key priority is capital recycling, with a target of approximately $2.0 billion from its Capital Release Unit (CRU) for the full year, aiming to reduce underlying gearing to 15% by year-end. The company is also implementing a cost savings program to achieve an additional $50 million in pre-tax run-rate savings in FY2026. Looking ahead to FY2027, Lendlease projects improved performance, with funds under management approaching $50 billion and Development completions of $4.5 billion.
