In response to the ongoing "Iran war in 2026," which began with US and Israeli airstrikes on February 28, 2026, and subsequent retaliatory strikes by Iran across the Middle East, Singapore has implemented several measures to ensure the safety and facilitate the return of its citizens in the affected region.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MFA) has issued comprehensive advisories for Singaporeans:
*   **Travel Deferral** Singaporeans are advised to defer all travel to Israel, Iran, and the wider Middle East due to the volatile security situation and disruptions to flights.
*   **Safety Precautions** Citizens currently in or transiting through the region are urged to take all necessary precautions, including staying indoors, seeking safe shelter when alerted, closely monitoring news, and adhering to the advice of local authorities.
*   **eRegister with MFA** Singaporeans abroad are strongly encouraged to e-register with the MFA to enable the ministry to contact them if necessary.
*   **Consular Assistance** Those requiring help can contact their nearest Singapore Overseas Mission or the 24-hour MFA Duty Office.

Furthermore, Singapore has initiated repatriation efforts:
*   **Repatriation Flights** On March 5, 2026, Singapore announced it would operate repatriation flights on March 7 and 8 to bring citizens home from the Middle East.
*   **Departure Points** Initial flights were scheduled from Muscat, Oman, primarily serving Singaporeans in Oman and the United Arab Emirates (UAE).
*   **Assisted Overland Trips** For citizens in Bahrain and Qatar, the MFA plans to arrange assisted overland travel to Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, where commercial flights remain operational.
*   **Ticket Costs** Tickets for these repatriation flights were priced at S$600 per seat.

Other recommendations from the MFA include considering alternative travel arrangements that avoid transiting through the affected region due to potential airspace closures or flight cancellations, and strongly advising the purchase of comprehensive travel insurance. Domestically, the Immigration & Checkpoints Authority Singapore has also implemented enhanced security measures at its checkpoints since February 28, 2026, in light of the heightened global security situation.
